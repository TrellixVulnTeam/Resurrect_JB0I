Our Mission

    People can pass away at any time, causing their friends and family to feel empty without them. Having a way to be reminded of them is a great way to adjust to life without them. While pictures and videos are already there, being able to have a conversation with them would be ideal.

Celebrities have a large social media presence, and through their social media, people gain a strong understanding of their personalities. However, having a conversation with them is still not possible, until now.

Find your Friends

We developed an AI algorithm using PyTorch and Tensorflow that requires users to input text conversations with someone. We will then process their request and return an AI adaptation that they can interact with. Users can also submit an audio clip of their friend, allowing the AI to speak with their voice. For those who have recently lost a friend or family member, this is a great coping mechanism to never lose memory of them.

Chat with Late Celebrities

We developed another AI algorithm that uses a Twitter web scraper to bring your favorite late celebrities back in an AI form. By interacting and conversing with the adaptation, you will experience a realistic interaction.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why should cities use our process?

Cities should use our process because currently, roads are very dangerous, and there isnâ€™t an accurate and efficient process to figure out where roads are risky and how to fix them. Local governments and city planners can use our software to improve their roads in a unique way. They are provided with a heatmap of where crashes in their city can occur and how to address those issues.

How do we collect data for our model?

We will receive data for our heatmaps from those who witnessed crashes as well as state/local governments. We have found that many cities already collect this data, but wonâ€™t release it publicly. Additionally, we would need cities to collect more data, and we have a system to allow residents to report crashes, improving our data collection process.

How can regular people use our website?

Residents can find a map with the hotspots of where crashes are likely. These heatmaps change based on an hourly basis and time of year to account for rush hours and temperature/weather. The common pedestrian or driver can also help improve the efficiency of our model by inputting data about crashes in their neighborhoods by interactively placing pins on the map, which we aggregate with already provided data.

How do we generate heatmaps?

Generating heatmaps relies on having both static and updated data. This includes where crashes occur as well as factors such as weather, road curvature, where signs are located, and more. Our model uses supervised learning to then generate coordinates on where it is most risky, which we then render using GeoPandas.
